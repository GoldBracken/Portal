Lead Developer Report:

Fara-Wayland Collection Site link Link to LeadDeveloper.md Link to Assignments.md Link to Communications.md Link to Roles.md

How did you manage your development team and comunicate the tasks for your junior devs?
I managed my development team by first assigning each developer a website that they would focus on the development of and then gave small sub tasks that they should also do, like checking links for validity and testing GitHub pages is displaying the website properly. Our main method of communication was discord, but they could see their roles and tasks in GitHub issues too.
How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?
The client and I mostly used discord, but we also communicated in person. There was no issues and the communications were good.
How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?
The client had created the base folder structure as outlined by the assignment, and from there I created any additional files that we needed. Then, the development team and I would push the changes to GitHub and I would send the link to the page via email.
How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?
GitHub works well most of the time, and I encountered no major issues with it. As long as I consistently pulled changes and pushed updates I made, the site ran smoothly.

General Experience and Reflection

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?
The most difficult part of the project for me was keeping all my responsibilities in order. It was hard for me to keep track of everything I was supposed to be doing between the four projects while also trying to maintain good communications with everyone I needed to. I felt I was able to overcome it to the best that I could, but there were things that were outside my control which was difficult to deal with.
What did you enjoy most about this project?
One thing that I enjoyed a lot about this project was programming the websites. I really like to program and it was fun building so many different kinds of pages at once. What would you do differently if you could do the project again?
I would try to have clearer expectations of those I worked with and ensure that our methods of communication were effective, fast, and open, so the products would be the best they could. Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.
On a scale of 1-10 of how confident I would be working on real-world projects, I would put myself at an 8. I feel confident in my ability to get what is expected of me done in a timely manner, but I feel like I need a bit more experience handling situations involving those I work with.
