How did you decide what content and images to use for your site plan?
When I was deciding what content and images to use, I wanted to focus on the purpose of the site. All the images and content should in some way support the overall message of the site. For instance, images should be relevant to the content message that is shared, which should also in some way relate to the sites main purpose of helping a user with finding a job or internship.

How did you communicate with your developer? Was there any issues with communication?
Early on, my developer and I established that email was the preffered method of communication. However, that meant that sometimes communication would be slow to be seen, and it was hard to confirm if they had seen the email unless they responded. This presented a small issue when I noticed the site was fairly lacking in content and CSS, along with most of the links being broken in some capacity. To ensure that this issue was addressed I eventually discussed it in person with the lead developer since I could not determine if he had even seen my email sent days prior.

Did your developer meet your expectations for your site?
At the time of writing this the answer would unfortunately be no. There are a couple pages that were almost entirely what I asked for, and I appreciate those that put in the effort to follow the site plan so exactly. However, two pages are essentially deviod of content, which is very disappointing. If there was a reason behind the missing pages, I was never informed of whether it just was neglected, or the person who was supposed to create the pages never did them, but in the end the fact that the home page is blank is rather saddening. Also discouraging was the fact that despite my direct intervention with the broken links and href and discussing them with the lead developer, almost none of the images are working properly at this time.

What will you do differently next time you hire a team to do a project for you?
I think somethings I would do differently next time I hire a team to do a project is look at their previous work to see if they make good websites and use a different method of communication. The communication method I would use would be faster, easier to check, and have more fluid communication design, like discord or google chat, where direct messages could quickly be seen and responded to.

How did you manage your development team and comunicate the tasks for your junior devs?
I managed my development team by first assigning each developer a page of the website that they would focus on the development of and then gave small sub tasks that they should also do, such as checking links for validity and testing if GitHub pages is displaying the website properly. Our main method of communication was discord, but they could see their roles and tasks in GitHub issues too. The advantage of using discord was we could quickly share information amongst the team members, and I also had created channels to reflect the Roles.md and Assignments.md, so they could quickly look at what was expected of them. This method proved effective and was helpful for catching mistakes and rectifying them early. I had made a mistake when I announced how the links for titles and banners should work, and one of the junior devs noticed the discrepancy between the wireframe and my statement. This meant that I could quickly change my previously incorrect announcement and then fix the incorrect html on subpage I was working on.

How did you communicate with your client? Was there any issues?
We first established how we would communicate going forward in person. After discussing some details and questions about the site initially, like the use of a stock image on the home page and how to get banner images for the subpages, we decided to use discord to communicate. This worked well and when a new junior developer was added, I was able to reach out and shortly the junior developer was also added to the projects repository as a collaborator. There were no issues with communication and if there was ever a question that arose, I often would ask in-person about it. Discord was more used for when something needed to be communicated outside of class and it was pressing or urgent, such as adding a new junior developer.

How did you handle publishing your clients site and sharing it with the client?
The publishing and creation of the site was handled by the client, and then from the GitHub pages deployement I took the link and posted it in the README.md file so it could more easily be accessed by the client and junior devs. The client had created the base folder structure as outlined by the assignment, and from there I created any additional files that we needed. Then, the development team and I would push the changes to GitHub and I would send the link to the page via discord.

How was your experience with managing your site with GitHub?
GitHub works well most of the time, and I encountered no major issues with the site deployed by the client or myself. Naturally, it usually took a couple minutes for the site to deploy when new changes were pushed to the site, but I was always able to access the website and see the contents.

How did you communicate with your team lead? Was there any issues?
The lead developer set up a discord server for the project they led. There were no issues in communications, any questions we had could just be asked in the chat and if the lead developer did not have an immediate answer, they would get back to us when they did. For example, I had a question about the CSS for price-graph and the lead dev said to use the CSS from the home page as a template.

Did you know what was expected of you for each site?
For the most part it was pretty straight forward. Like most of the projects, each person was assigned to a specific page, which means they were in charge of the html and CSS for their page. I was assigned the price-graph page. However, one difficulty I encountered was the initial wireframe I was using provided from the canvas discussion was outdated. I was not made aware until I asked since I could not initially find the wireframe for price-graph. However, after asking about the missing wireframe, I was informed by the lead dev that there was an updated site plan and after that it was sorted out.

Were you able to contribute to your sites?
I was able to contribute to the html and CSS of the sites I was working on. On this site I contributed to the html for the price-graph.html file and price-graph.css file.

Did you run into any issues with GitHub and adding your changes to the sites?
I occasionally ran into a bug with GitHub where the CSS would not always update properly. This usually seemed to happen when I would push an update that had changes to CSS and html but it was not always consistent. I found I could fix it by pushing a change with only CSS modifications in most cases.

General Experience and Reflection

What was the most challenging part of this project for you? Were you able to overcome it?
The most difficult part of the project for me was keeping all my responsibilities in order. It was hard for me to keep track of everything I was supposed to be doing between the four projects while also trying to maintain good communications with everyone I needed to. I felt I was able to overcome it to the best that I could, but there were things that were outside my control which was difficult to deal with.

What did you enjoy most about this project?
One thing that I enjoyed a lot about this project was programming the websites. I really like to program and it was fun building so many different kinds of pages at once.

What would you do differently if you could do the project again?
I would try to have clearer expectations of those I worked with and ensure that our methods of communication were effective, fast, and open, so the products would be the best they could.

Do you feel more confident in working on real-world projects after this experience? Explain.
On a scale of 1-10 of how confident I would be working on real-world projects, I would put myself at an 8. I feel confident in my ability to get what is expected of me done in a timely manner, but I feel like I need a bit more experience handling situations involving those I work with.
